# Problem Statement

## Background

The rapid growth of digital transformation has significantly increased the amount of sensitive data handled by organizations.

While cybersecurity solutions such as firewalls, IDS, IPS, and SIEM are effective against external attacks, insider threats remain one of the most difficult security challenges.

Insiders already possess legitimate credentials and authorized access to organizational resources. As a result, malicious actions often appear similar to normal business activities.

According to multiple industry reports, insider incidents can result in:

- Data breaches
- Intellectual property theft
- Financial losses
- Regulatory violations
- Reputational damage

## Existing Challenges

Current solutions mainly focus on:

- Signature-based detection
- Rule-based alerting
- Isolated anomaly detection

However, these approaches have several limitations:

- Lack of personalized behavioral profiles
- Limited contextual understanding
- High false positive rate
- Insufficient forensic evidence protection

## Proposed Solution

This research proposes InsiderGuard AI, a platform integrating:

1. Digital Behavior Twin
2. User and Entity Behavior Analytics (UEBA)
3. Data Loss Prevention (DLP)
4. Blockchain-backed Evidence Preservation
5. AI-assisted Investigation

to improve insider threat detection and investigation capabilities.

## Expected Contributions

- Personalized behavioral modeling
- Multi-source anomaly detection
- Evidence integrity verification
- Automated incident investigation
